# Mahjong Solitaire: Official Rules & Gameplay Guide

Welcome to the comprehensive guide for **Mahjong Solitaire** (also known as Shanghai Solitaire). This is a classic single-player matching game that uses a standard set of 144 Mahjong tiles arranged in a distinctive layered layout (most traditionally the "Turtle" or "Pyramid" shape). 

The goal of the game is simple yet challenging: clear the board by matching identical pairs of "free" tiles.

---

## 1. The Game Components & Tiles
A standard Mahjong Solitaire game is played with a set of **144 tiles**, divided into several distinct suits and special categories:

### Suit Tiles (108 Tiles Total)
*   **Dots / Circles (筒子):** Numbered 1 through 9 (4 of each = 36 tiles)
*   **Bamboo (條子 / 条子):** Numbered 1 through 9 (4 of each = 36 tiles). Note: The 1-of-Bamboo is traditionally depicted as a sparrow or bird.
*   **Characters (萬子 / 万子):** Numbered 1 through 9 (4 of each = 36 tiles)

### Honor Tiles (28 Tiles Total)
*   **Winds (風):** East (東), South (南), West (西), North (北) (4 of each = 16 tiles)
*   **Dragons (箭):** Red Dragon (中), Green Dragon (發), White Dragon (白 - often blank or framed) (4 of each = 12 tiles)

### Special / Bonus Tiles (8 Tiles Total)
These tiles are unique because they do **not** require an exact match. You can match any tile within the same subgroup:
*   **Seasons (季):** Spring (春), Summer (夏), Autumn (秋), Winter (冬) (1 of each = 4 tiles)
    *   *Match rule:* Any Season tile matches with any other Season tile.
*   **Flowers (花):** Plum (梅), Orchid (蘭), Bamboo (竹), Chrysanthemum (菊) (1 of each = 4 tiles)
    *   *Match rule:* Any Flower tile matches with any other Flower tile.

---

## 2. Setting Up the Board
*   **The Layout:** The tiles are arranged in a 3D multi-layered structure. The most common layout is the **Turtle** (or Pyramid), which features 5 distinct layers.
*   **Layer Breakdown (Turtle Layout):**
    *   *Base (Layer 1):* The largest, flat layer of tiles on the bottom.
    *   *Layers 2, 3, & 4:* Stacked on top in progressively smaller configurations.
    *   *Layer 5 (Peak):* A single tile placed at the absolute center top.

---

## 3. Core Gameplay Rules

### The Concept of "Free" (or Unlocked) Tiles
You can only select and remove a tile if it is **"free"**. A tile is considered free if it meets **both** of the following conditions:
1.  **No tiles are resting on top of it:** It must be completely uncovered. Even a slight overlap from a higher layer locks the tile.
2.  **It has an open lateral side:** It must have either its left side **or** its right side completely clear of other tiles. If a tile is blocked on both its left and right sides, it cannot be played, even if its top and bottom sides are completely open.

### Matching and Removing Tiles
1.  Locate two free tiles that are identical (or compatible Special tiles).
2.  Select the first tile, then select the second tile.
3.  If they match, they will be removed from the board, revealing the tiles underneath or adjacent to them.
4.  If they do not match, the first tile will be deselected.

---

## 4. Special Matching Rules
*   **Standard Tiles (Suits, Winds, Dragons):** Must be an exact, identical match. For example, a *3 of Bamboo* can only match with another *3 of Bamboo*. A *Red Dragon* can only match with another *Red Dragon*.
*   **Seasons:** Can match with any other Season tile. 
    *   *Example:* Spring can match with Winter.
*   **Flowers:** Can match with any other Flower tile. 
    *   *Example:* Plum can match with Orchid.

---

## 5. Winning and Losing

### Winning Condition
*   You win the game when you successfully clear **all 144 tiles** from the board.

### Losing Condition (Game Over)
*   You lose (or get stuck) when there are still tiles left on the board, but **no legal matches can be made** with the remaining free tiles.
*   *Note on Solvability:* Because the tiles are shuffled randomly at the start, not every generated board is solvable.

---

## 6. Common Video Game Assist Features
Most modern Mahjong Solitaire video games include helpful tools to keep the gameplay smooth and enjoyable:

*   **Hint System:** Highlights a pair of matching free tiles if you are stuck.
*   **Undo:** Allows you to reverse your previous moves. This is highly strategic, as removing a pair might block a better path later on.
*   **Shuffle / Reshuffle:** Re-arranges the remaining tiles on the board into the current layout shape without changing their positions, opening up new matching possibilities when you run out of moves.
*   **Show Playable / Dim Locked Tiles:** A visual aid that dims all locked tiles, making it immediately obvious which tiles are currently free.
